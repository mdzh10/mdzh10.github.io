An Optimized YOLOv5 Based Approach For Real-time Vehicle Detection At Road Intersections Using Fisheye Cameras

Md. Jahin Alam1, Muhammad Zubair Hasan2, Md Maisoon Rahman3, Md Awsafur Rahman4, Najibul Haque Sarker5, Shariar Azad6, Tasnim Nishat Islam7, Bishmoy Paul8, Tanvir Anjum9, Barproda Halder10, Shaikh Anowarul Fattah11

Optimized YOLOv5 approach for real-time vehicle detection at road intersections using fisheye cameras with 13.7% improvement in mAP @ 0.5.

Abstract

Real time vehicle detection is a challenging task for urban traffic surveillance. Increase in urbanization leads to increase in accidents and traffic congestion in junction areas resulting in delayed travel time. In order to solve these problems, an intelligent system utilizing automatic detection and tracking system is significant.

But this becomes a challenging task at road intersection areas which require a wide range of field view. For this reason, fish eye cameras are widely used in real time vehicle detection purpose to provide large area coverage and 360 degree view at junctions. However, it introduces challenges such as light glare from vehicles and street lights, shadow, non-linear distortion, scaling issues of vehicles and proper localization of small vehicles.

To overcome each of these challenges, a modified YOLOv5 object detection scheme is proposed. YOLOv5 is a deep learning oriented convolutional neural network (CNN) based object detection method. The proposed scheme for detecting vehicles in fish-eye images consists of a light-weight day-night CNN classifier so that two different solutions can be implemented to address the day-night detection issues.

Methodology

Our approach combines a lightweight day-night CNN classifier with an optimized YOLOv5 model to address the unique challenges of fisheye camera vehicle detection, including light glare, shadows, and non-linear distortion.

Results

Our optimized YOLOv5 model achieves a 13.7% improvement in mAP @ 0.5 over the baseline YOLOv5 model on the ISSD VIP Cup dataset, demonstrating superior performance in challenging fisheye camera conditions.

Technical Details

Model Architecture

The proposed system consists of a lightweight day-night classifier followed by an optimized YOLOv5 detection model, specifically designed to handle the challenges of fisheye camera imagery including distortion correction and multi-scale vehicle detection.
