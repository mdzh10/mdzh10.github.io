Exploring Challenges and Innovations in E-Commerce Recommendation Systems: A Comprehensive Review

Mashfiqur Rahman1, Samiul Mushfik2, Makhdum Ahsan Rupak3, Muhammad Zubair Hasan4, Minhaz Bin Farukee5, Shawrup Kumer Suter6
1Vanguard Australia, Melbourne, Australia 2Stibo DX, Dhaka, Bangladesh 3Kona Software Lab Ltd, Dhaka, Bangladesh 4Celloscope, Dhaka, Bangladesh 5ACI Ltd, Dhaka, Bangladesh 6Delivery Hero, Berlin, Germany
Paper Springer

Comprehensive Review of e-commerce recommendation systems addressing challenges and innovative approaches in the digital marketplace.

Abstract

Recommendation systems play a pivotal role in the digital age, with ongoing research focused on enhancing their effectiveness. This paper delves into the common challenges associated with developing these systems, including the cold-start problem, handling sparse datasets, and the use of matrix filling in hierarchical methods.

We explore innovative approaches that include the integration of diverse algorithms and the application of alternative techniques, such as deep learning. Our research aims to establish an empirically based standard for various aspects of recommendation systems, thereby serving as a valuable reference for future studies.

The comprehensive review addresses key challenges in e-commerce recommendation systems, including collaborative filtering limitations, content-based filtering approaches, and hybrid methods that combine multiple techniques for improved performance and user experience.

Methodology

Our comprehensive review methodology encompasses systematic analysis of existing recommendation system approaches, evaluation of their effectiveness in e-commerce contexts, and identification of innovative solutions to common challenges.

Key Findings

Our analysis reveals critical insights into recommendation system performance, highlighting the importance of addressing cold-start problems and the effectiveness of hybrid approaches in e-commerce applications.

System Architecture

The review covers various recommendation system architectures including collaborative filtering, content-based filtering, and hybrid approaches, analyzing their strengths and limitations in e-commerce environments.
